Help! I have no idea what quantum computing is.

Dr. Talia Gershon explains quantum computing for WIRED (2018)

https://youtu.be/OWJCfOvochA
Dr. Gershon is the director of research strategy and growth initiatives at IBM. Here she not only expertly explains quantum computing to five levels of understanding—personified by a child, a teen, undergrad, grad student, and a professional—but also refers to an actual quantum computer apparatus that she’s brought with her to the dialogues. I have great respect for those who can take rather complex subjects and make them intuitive. It is incredibly difficult to do. Dr. Gershon makes it look easy.

Help—I am a programmer.

Andrew Helwer’s “Quantum computing for computer scientists” lecture for Microsoft Research (2018)

https://youtu.be/F_Riqjdh2oM
Named after the book of the same title—a book Andrew reveres—this lecture explains qubits as matrices, gate operations as matrices, superposition, and demonstrates an example of quantum computing’s exponential power over classical computing. This is the video that got me started on building Q.js. I’ve watched it about 100 times, and each time I’m able to gleen a sliver of new insight.

Quantum computation and quantum information (2000)

http://mmrc.amss.cas.cn/tlb/201702/W020170224608149940643.pdf
This tome is credited to Michael Nielsen and Isaac Chuang—sometimes referred to as “Mike & Ike” for short. It is the must-have, foundational book for quantum computing. Get this book. Beware: It is full of maths symbols. As such I’ve found it useful to breeze-read through some sections, totally unconcerned about if I’m truly absorbing the material or not. Then to go back and read it again. And again. And yet again. And so on. By employing this unanxious repetition across days / moods / contexts it is almost by accident that the puzzle pieces begin to join and the picture comes in to focus.

Playing with the Bloch Sphere (2019)

https://quantumcomputing.stackexchange.com/questions/4720/what-circuit-or-operation-corresponds-to-the-tensor-product
Published just days after I began work on Q.js, this blog post by Gwilym Newton of IBM solved a problem I was banging my head against the wall over: How to properly convert a qubit’s alpha and beta properties in to phi and theta angles! If you’re building your own Bloch Sphere visualizer you’ll notice this is surprisingly difficult to find code examples of. This helped me a lot.
