Playground

This page is your behind-the-curtain look at interface ideas in development. While the code API already functions (browse the other pages for details) some functionality here may be more limited than implied. For example, the interactive circuit does not yet allow editing of multi-register operations. (They will be removed if edited.) Also coming soon: revised version of operation copying / pasting.

Plain text input

Type, paste, or edit your circuit here to generate an interactive circuit diagram below. You can learn more about Q’s plain text format in “Writing quatum circuits.”

Interactive circuit diagram

Drag and drop quantum gates from this gates palette on to the circuit diagram just below it. You can also drag gates around within the circuit diagram itself. Editing the diagram will recalculate the results below as well as re-export the circuit to various formats.

Sample circuits

This page previously contained the following pre-loaded sample circuits for experimenting with. Their plain text representations are included here for reference. (You can copy and paste their text in to the input field at the top of the page to load them.)

Bell state

Here’s a Bell state (which produces quantum entanglement), but with Pauli X operations added to both registers as a finale—so the only two possible results are 00 and 11; all or nothing! 


H X#0 X
I X#1 X

Whiplash

This monster demonstrates some overlapping mult-register operations and an “upside-down CNOT” finale. Notice too how in the diagram version the boxes within each column (moment) are equal widths and there’s an attempt to center the text labels within their boxes. Also note how the overlapping connectors are dealt with, eh? Of course this circuit would make little sense in the real world. It’s here purely to test different aspects of rendering a circuit visually. 


H  X.0#0  Y    Z    X#1
H  X.1#0  X#0  I    X#0
H  X.0#1  Y    X#0  S
H  X      X#1  X#1  I
H  X.1#1  Y    Z    S
